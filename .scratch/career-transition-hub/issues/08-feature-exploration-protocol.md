# Feature exploration protocol

Type: grilling
Status: resolved
Blocked by:

## Question

How do **feature exploration** spikes graduate (or get killed) relative to the production Day-1 floor?

Lock a lightweight protocol: when to open a prototype/research ticket, what “keep for Day 1” vs “post Day 1” vs “out of scope” means, and how exploration is prevented from infinitely delaying the robust Day-1 cut. Not a backlog of features — the rules for the backlog.

## Answer

### What a “spike” is

A **short, timeboxed dig** to answer one uncertainty — research note or throwaway prototype — **not** a feature ship. About one session.

### Protocol (human confirmed 2026-07-09)

**When to spike:**  
- Allowed for **free curiosity**, not only Day-1 design holes.  
- Also fine when stuck on a real decision.

**Graduation (minimal):**  
- **Build in Day-1** / **Later** / **Don’t build** — enough. Fancy label tables optional.

**WIP limit:**  
- **None.** Human may open as many explorations as they want.

**Session close (stamped, human OK):**  
- Aim to finish a spike in **one session**, then close it (later / don’t build / or promote to Day-1).  
- Prefer not to leave eternal half-claimed digs — but no hard WIP cap.

**Anti-delay (still true without WIP limit):**  
- Spikes **do not block** scaffold / core Day-1 build unless you explicitly decide a spike outcome is required for the floor.  
- “Would be cool” defaults to **Later**, not Day-1, unless you say otherwise.
